Air Conditioner Types

Split Air Conditioner

Modern split AC with powerful cooling and energy efficiency.A Split Air Conditioner (AC) is a cooling system that consists of two separate units: an indoor unit and an outdoor unit. The indoor unit is installed inside the room and contains the evaporator coil and fan, which blow cool air into the room. The outdoor unit, placed outside the house, contains the compressor and condenser, which release the heat absorbed from indoors.

Non-Inverter Air Conditioner

Compact and affordable AC suitable for big rooms.A Non-Inverter Air Conditioner is a traditional type of AC where the compressor operates at a fixed speed. When the room temperature rises above the set level, the compressor turns on at full capacity to cool the room, and once the desired temperature is reached, it completely switches off. This cycle of starting and stopping continues repeatedly, which can lead to higher electricity consumption compared to inverter ACs.

Inverter Air Conditioner

Silent operation with maximum power saving.An Inverter Air Conditioner (AC) is a modern type of AC where the compressor can adjust its speed according to the room’s temperature. Unlike non-inverter ACs, which turn the compressor fully on or off, inverter ACs run continuously at variable speeds, using only the energy needed to maintain the desired temperature. This results in more consistent cooling, quieter operation, and significant energy savings. Inverter ACs are slightly more expensive upfront than non-inverter models, but they are cost-effective in the long run due to lower electricity bills
